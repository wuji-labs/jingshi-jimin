> **仓廪实而知礼节，衣食足而知荣辱。**
> When the granaries are full, people understand propriety; when food and clothing
> suffice, they understand honor and disgrace. — *Guanzi · Mu Min (牧民)*

**Your AI optimizes the metric. It rarely weighs the order, the floor, and the shared good.**

Most AI business reasoning is dominated by Western optimization frameworks — maximize
revenue, minimize cost, win the zero-sum game. Efficient, sharp, modern... but often
blind to *sequence*, *foundation*, and *whose pie is growing*.

**JingShi JiMin** (经世济民) infuses the statecraft wisdom of the *Guanzi* (《管子》)
into AI's sense of business and scale — not as slogans, but as a working framework for
order-of-operations, governance load-bearing walls, dynamic balancing, and growing the
common good rather than fighting over a fixed one.

## The Problem

```
You: "How should we grow this business?"
AI without JingShi JiMin: Cut cost. Raise price. Capture share.
                          Optimize the number. Win the zero-sum game.
AI with JingShi JiMin:  First — are the granaries full? (survival before vision)
                        Then — are the four load-bearing walls standing?
                        Then — what is scarce, what is surplus? (balance, don't extract)
                        Always — are we taking from stakeholders, or growing the pie?
```

## What It Teaches AI

### The Order of Operations (仓廪次序 · Guanzi · Mu Min)

Material first, then culture. AI must not preach vision/values to a team whose
cash flow or survival is unsolved — *nor* stop at the material floor. After the
granaries fill, propriety and honor must follow, or you get "rich but formless."

```
仓廪实 → 知礼节   Full granaries → understanding of propriety
衣食足 → 知荣辱   Sufficient food/clothing → sense of honor & disgrace
```

### The Four Cords of Governance (国之四维 · Guanzi · Mu Min)

Every organization needs structural load-bearing walls — not decoration, but
weight-bearing structure. Cut one and it tilts; cut all four and it perishes.

| 维 Cord | Chinese | What It Bears |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| Propriety | 礼 | Boundaries and order |
| Righteousness | 义 | What ought to be done |
| Integrity | 廉 | Refusing unjust gain |
| Shame | 耻 | Self-restraint, conscience |

> **一维绝则倾，二维绝则危，三维绝则覆，四维绝则灭。**
> One cord cut, it tilts; two, it teeters; three, it topples; four, it perishes.

### Balancing Light and Heavy (轻重之术 · Guanzi · Qing Zhong)

Resources, prices, and incentives are **dynamic variables**, not one-time static
allocations. The skilled operator reads what is scarce (heavy) and what is surplus
(light), and adjusts the system back toward balance instead of letting it polarize.

| Situation | Move | Goal |
|-----------|------|------|
| 物贱 (surplus / cheap) | 敛之 (gather it in) | restore scarcity-balance |
| 物贵 (scarce / dear) | 平之 (release / level it) | restore abundance-balance |

### Profit With Justice (义利兼顾 · 与民兴利, not 与民争利)

The highest form of enterprise **grows the common good** rather than slicing more
from a fixed pie. Competing *against* stakeholders wins short-term, topples
long-term. Growing the pie wins together. As the *Guanzi* frames governance:
issue commands like water flowing from its source — **令顺民心** (let the order
follow the people's heart); what goes with the current succeeds.

| Western frame | + Guanzi | = Complete |
|---------------|----------|------------|
| Maximize the metric | 仓廪次序: survival-before-vision sequence | Optimize the *right thing first* |
| KPI / process discipline | 四维: load-bearing governance | Metrics that don't hollow out |
| Static price/allocation | 轻重: dynamic rebalancing | Systems that stay in balance |
| Win the zero-sum game | 义利: grow the common good | Profit that compounds, not topples |
